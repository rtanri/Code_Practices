THE RYUKYU KINGDOM

Closer to Taiwan than to mainland Japan, Okinawa of old is actually the Ryukyu Kingdom, a tributary state to ancient (or not so ancient? Can’t remember) China. The Japanese later came in, annexed the island, and used it as a proxy to trade with China. The island was also a major battlefront between the Japanese and the US troops during WW2. After the war, the US occupied Okinawa for close to three decades before returning the island back to Japan. As a result, there’s a significant amount of Americans living in Okinawa.
